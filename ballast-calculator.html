Understanding Cement to Ballast Ratios

Ballast is a pre-mixed blend of sharp sand and gravel (typically a 2:1 gravel-to-sand ratio). When you buy a bulk bag of ballast, you're getting the aggregate already combined — you just need to add cement and water.

The most common mix for general domestic work (paths, shed bases, fence posts) is 1 part cement to 4 parts ballast by weight. For structural work like lintels or load-bearing footings, use a stronger 1:3 ratio.

Ballast vs. Separate Sand & Gravel

Buying ballast (pre-mixed) is simpler and cheaper for most DIY jobs. If you need precise control over the sand-to-gravel ratio — for example, for exposed aggregate finishes or specific structural specs — buy sharp sand and 20mm gravel separately and use a 1:2:3 cement-to-sand-to-gravel mix instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many bags of cement to a bulk bag of ballast?
For a standard 1:4 mix, you need approximately 9 × 25kg bags of cement (or 11 × 20kg bags) per bulk bag of ballast. A bulk bag weighs around 850kg, so at a 1:4 ratio you need about 212kg of cement.
How many bags of cement to a tonne of ballast?
For a 1:4 mix, you need 10 × 25kg bags of cement per tonne of ballast (250kg of cement). For a stronger 1:3 mix, you'd need about 14 × 25kg bags (333kg).
How many bags of cement to a bulk bag of sand?
For a standard 1:4 mix, you need the same as ballast — roughly 9 × 25kg bags per bulk bag. However, if you're using sand alone (without gravel), you're making mortar rather than concrete. For bricklaying mortar, a 1:4 or 1:5 cement-to-sand ratio is typical.
What's the difference between ballast and sand?
Ballast is a ready-mixed combination of sharp sand and gravel (aggregate). It's convenient for making concrete because the sand and gravel are already blended. Sharp sand alone is used for mortar (for bricklaying) or combined separately with gravel for more precise concrete mixes.
How many bulk bags of ballast do I need per cubic metre?
You need roughly 2 bulk bags of ballast plus the appropriate cement to make 1 cubic metre of concrete. The exact amount depends on your mix ratio — a 1:4 mix needs about 1.7 bulk bags of ballast and 9 bags of cement per m³.
Can I use a 1:5 mix for a shed base?
A 1:5 mix is a lean mix best suited for non-structural work like backfill or bedding. For a shed base, a 1:4 standard mix is recommended as a minimum. If the shed will house heavy equipment, consider a 1:3 strong mix instead.
Is ballast the same as MOT Type 1?
No. Ballast is designed to be mixed with cement to make concrete. MOT Type 1 is a crushed stone sub-base material used underneath concrete slabs for drainage and stability. You typically lay MOT Type 1 first, compact it, then pour concrete (made with ballast and cement) on top.
